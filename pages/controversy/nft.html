NFTs

A non-fungible token (NFT) is a unique digital identifier that cannot be copied, substituted, or subdivided,
that is recorded in a blockchain, and that is used to certify authenticity and ownership.
The ownership of an NFT is recorded in the blockchain and can be transferred by the owner,
allowing NFTs to be sold and traded. NFTs can be created by anybody, and require few or no coding skills to create. (See more about NFTs)

Digital art is a common use case for NFTs. High-profile auctions of NFTs linked to digital art have received considerable public attention.
The work entitled "Merge" by artist Pak was the most expensive NFT, with an auction price of US$91.8 million.

Some NFT collections, including Bored Apes, EtherRocks, and CryptoPunks are examples of generative art,
where many different images are created by assembling a selection of simple picture components in different combinations.
Generative art, by itself, fulfills the criteria of the "lack of value" of digital art, being randomly generated by an algorythm
using different premade assets ("parts" of an illustration). Meaning you can get a wide "variety" of "unique" illustrations out of a small amount of assets.
Besides, no centralized means of authentication exists to prevent stolen and counterfeit digital works from being sold as NFTs,
leaving a wide breach for art theft and scams.
